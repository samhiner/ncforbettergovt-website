Our Story

NC for Better Government is a non-partisan organization dedicated to mobilizing constituents to advocate to their state legislators in support of the abolition of lame-duck power grabs. Sam Hiner was captivated when he learned of lame-duck power grabs. Sam researched the issue and knew it could be solved with legislative action. In an effort to make a change, Sam spoke to state legislators and while as a Senate Page, advocated for solutions to the issue. However, Sam realized to make real change, he would need the support of local citizens across NC. He decided that any change would require a grassroots movement, getting the people to demand a better democracy. Knowing of Mick Tobin’s success with running a non-profit organization and their experience working together to organize local blood drives, Sam approached Mick about making a change. Together, they created an organization to educate and mobilize citizens in NC on lame-duck power grabs. This became what is today NC for Better Government.

People

Sam Hiner - Founder/President

Sam is a rising senior at the North Carolina School of Science and Math in Durham, NC. There, he serves his fellow students as the President of the Student Senate. Sam is also a part of his school’s All Politics is Local Club, which conducts outreach between his school and the state legislature.

In his free time, Sam volunteers for the Durham Teen Court as a student attorney and hosts blood drives as a part of the Red Cross Leaders Save Lives program. Sam is also a Political Affairs Intern with [The Borgen Project], where he lobbies his Congressional Leaders to support life-saving programs to improve conditions for those suffering from extreme poverty.

Sam is passionate about using his time to do as much good as possible. He believes that he can have the most impact by helping end extreme poverty, which he pursues through the Borgen Project, and by pushing for good-government reforms, which is why he started NC for Better Government.

Sam is the Founder and President of NC for Better Government. In this role, he works with state legislators to build support for our mission and develop policy, codes the website, and recruits and manages volunteers.


Mick Tobin - Vice President

Mick is a rising senior at South Iredell Highschool. He loves to play the saxophone in jazz band, compete on the varsity tennis team, and serve his community as a Coach at Chick-Fil-A Mooresville.

Mick loves helping others. Since being an IB student since the age of twelve, he has completed over 200 hours of community service. As a Red Cross Blood Drive Leader, Docent at the Ada Jenkins Center, and regular community volunteer at Saint Therese Catholic Church, Mick is dedicated to serving his community.

Mick is passionate about business. He is the President and Founder of South Iredell’s FBLA chapter, which is an active member of the Lake Norman Chamber of Commerce. Mick is also a marketing intern at Willis Towers Watson, a global financial risk-management company. Additionally, Mick is the President of the nonprofit business, Sweetpuddles [Link to Sweetpuddles]. Mick is the President of Management and Daily Operations and manages the professional outreach methods, development of the products/services, and strategies to keep the team unified and focused.

Mick is the Vice President of NC For Better Government. In addition to aiding Sam on vital tasks, he focuses on methods for community outreach, strategies for growth, and ensures the standards of NC For Better Government are always upheld in projects and by volunteers.
